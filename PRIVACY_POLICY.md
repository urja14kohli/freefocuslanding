# Privacy Policy for Lockdin

**Last updated: January 4, 2026**

## Overview

Lockdin is a browser extension designed to help you stay focused by blocking distracting websites. We are committed to protecting your privacy.

## Data Collection

**We do not collect any personal data.**

Lockdin stores the following information **locally on your device only**:

- Your current focus task (e.g., "Masters Applications")
- Your list of blocked websites

This data is stored using Chrome's local storage API (`chrome.storage.local`) and **never leaves your device**.

## Data Sharing

We do not:
- Collect personal information
- Track your browsing history
- Send any data to external servers
- Use analytics or tracking tools
- Share any information with third parties

## Permissions Explained

| Permission | Why We Need It |
|------------|----------------|
| `storage` | To save your focus task and blocked sites list locally |
| `<all_urls>` | To check if the current website should be blocked |

## Data Retention

All data remains on your device until you:
- Clear your browser data
- Uninstall the extension
- Manually remove items from the blocked list

## Changes to This Policy

If we update this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date above.

## Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please open an issue on our GitHub repository or contact us through the Chrome Web Store listing.

---

**TL;DR**: Lockdin stores everything locally on your device. We don't collect, track, or share anything. Your data stays yours.
